How to make Jollof rice

Description

This is a foolproof recipe for cooking Jollof rice, West Africa’s most popular dish. I like to serve it as a side with my West African-style grilled beef (Suya), but it can also be a main course.

Ingredients

  • Tomato Sauce:
    1. 1 large , ripe tomato, cored and quartered
    2. 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
    3. 1 whole habanero pepper, stem removed
    4. 1 whole Fresno chili, or jalapeño, stem removed
    5. 4 cloves garlic, peeled
    6. 1 tablespoon minced ginger
    7. 2 cups water
  • Rice
    1. ⅓ cup olive oil
    2. 1 red onion, diced
    3. 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
    4. ¼ cup tomato paste
    5. 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
    6. 1 teaspoon curry powder
    7. 1 teaspoon cumin
    8. 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    9. ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    10. ¼ teaspoon turmeric
    11. 2 ¼ cups basmati rice
    12. 1 bay leaf
    13. 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon paste
    14. ¼ cup chopped green onionfor garnish
      (optional)
    15. ¼ cup chopped cilantro, for garnish
      (optional)

Steps to make Jollof rice

  1. Step 1:

    2. Combine tomato, red bell pepper, habanero pepper, fresno chili, garlic, ginger, and water in a blender for the sauce. Blend on high until smooth and set aside.

  2. Step 2:

    3. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and salt and sauté until onion starts to soften, 4 to 5 minutes. Clear space in the middle of the pan, add tomato paste, and sauté for 2 to 3 minutes. Season with paprika, curry powder, cumin, thyme, pepper, and turmeric; continue cooking for 1 to 2 minutes until everything is well combined.

  3. Step 3:

    4. Turn off the heat, and stir in the rice, making sure every grain of rice is coated with the oil-tomato mixture. Add bay leaf and chicken bouillon paste.

  4. Step 4:

    5. Turn heat to high, stir in tomato sauce, and bring to a vigorous simmer. Cover with a tight fitting lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for 20 minutes. Do not remove the lid or stir rice.

  5. Step 5

    6. Turn off heat after 20 minutes, and set the timer for 12 minutes. Allow rice to sit and do not remove the lid.

  6. Step 6

    7. After the 12 minutes are up, remove lid, and fluff rice with a fork, separating all the grains. Serve garnished with green onions and chopped cilantro.

Cooks Notes:

If rice seems to be a little undercooked at this point, stop fluffing. Pat the rice down gently with the back of the fork, cover tightly again, and set heat to medium low. Cook for another 5 minutes, and check again.
